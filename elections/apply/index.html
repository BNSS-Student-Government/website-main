How To Apply

Be sure to read each step carefully!

  • Step 1

    Attend the information session

    Attend the Zoom information session on March 1st at 5:00 PM. Student Government members will be providing information on the positions and the application process.

    If you missed the information session, you can find the slideshow here.

  • Step 2

    Research the possible positions

    Students should familiarize themselves with the positions of Student Government. Students should ask questions and decide which position would best suit their strengths and interests.

  • Step 3

    Fill out the election package

    Complete election package. Fill out the basic information, answer the written questions, obtain signatures from peers and request for two teacher referrals. Complete the package by March 10th. Submit to either the main office or to our email.


    Download Election Package

  • Step 4

    Candidates are notified

    If the student is chosen as a candidate, they will be notified by the end of the day on March 12th. They will then be allowed to prepare their campaign platform.

  • Step 5

    Future steps

    As a chosen candidate, they will be requested to complete additional material, including a Candidate Board as well as a short campaign video. From there on, they will be organizing their campaign platform and preparing for election week (April 12th - April 16th).

    See Campaign for more information.


Please note: If you are applying for an appointed position, you will not need to campaign. You will receive a separate email regarding your interview date when candidates are released.

President

This is an elected position.

The President of Burnaby North’s Student Government chairs both general and executive meetings. The President also drafts new event proposals and acts as the student liaison with the administration.

  • Eligible Applicants: Current Grade 11s
Questions? Contact the current co-presidents!

Vice President

This is an elected position.

The Vice President is responsible for overseeing the logistical plans for Student Government meetings and events. They assume the Presidential roles should they be indisposed or unavailable. The Vice President chairs meetings for the United Federation of Clubs.

  • Eligible Applicants: Current Grade 11s
Questions? Contact the current co-vice presidents!
  • Henry Xi | Makayla Young

Secretary

This is an elected position.

The Secretary creates agendas, records minutes, and distributes them among the executives and administration for Student Government meetings. While managing the schedule and contents, the Secretary acts to organize the efficient workflow of the entire Student Government team.

  • Eligible Applicants: Current Grade 11s
Questions? Contact the current secretary!
  • Ethan Eng

Treasurer

The Treasurer manages all monetary elements by working directly with administration to oversee the Student Government budget. The treasurer works in close relations to Mrs. Goss, thus requiring an earnest determination to learn and connect.

Indigenous Rep

This is an appointed position.

The Indigenous Representative is the liaison with both the Indigenous student body and teachers. The Indigenous Representative informs the Indigenous community of Student Government events at their student meetings while also representing their needs to Student Government and administration.

Unfortunately, we do not have an Indigenous Representative on our team this year. If you have any questions, please contact Student Government directly. Thank you!

They also prepare for their graduation year by planning and organizing fundraisers.
